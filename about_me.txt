I'm a data practitioner based in Berlin,
with most of my work over the last 8+ years
centred around analytics engineering. I've touched 
most things across the data spectrum, from data 
science to pipeline building, but I found myself 
gravitating towards something more specific: 
modelling data, structuring analytical warehouses, 
and building the toolset around them using 
the modern data stack.

Nowadays, most of my time goes to the most 
life changing, character building experience 
I've had so far: a little human being who showed up
and turned our whole world upside down in a matter 
of months. Beyond the incredible depth of feeling 
and the completely new perspective on life, it 
teaches me something very practical too. What being
actually busy means. How to use every tiny window of
free time with real focus and intention. How to 
prioritise when you suddenly have a very clear 
purpose to do everything better.

In the data world, I'm currently most interested in
how AI will reshape the analytics pipeline and
semantic layer. Not just the tooling, but the deeper
question: how to bring true purpose and meaning to 
decisions made with data.

Sometimes I make music and work on a couple of
musical projects. It's mostly been a hands off
keyboards, no screen experience for me, a very
different aspect of life and doing. But nowadays
I'm looking for where technology and my musical work
might intersect.

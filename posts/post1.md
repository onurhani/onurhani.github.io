# How To Develop A Profitable App

*12 Jan 2020*

---

The goal of this project was to analyse data from the App Store and Google Play Store to understand what types of apps are likely to attract the most users — because in these markets, revenue is primarily driven by in-app ads, meaning more users equals more revenue.

The dataset included information on app categories, ratings, reviews, size, pricing, and installs across both platforms. I focused on free apps, since those are the ones that rely on ad-driven revenue models.

**Key findings:**

- Apps in the **Books and Reference**, **Communication**, and **Social** categories showed the highest average install counts across both stores.
- The Google Play Store had significantly more apps and installs than the App Store, making it a better starting platform for reaching a wide audience.
- Many categories appeared saturated with low-quality apps — an opportunity for well-built alternatives that deliver real value.
- Games had the highest volume of apps, but also the most competition and lowest average rating.

The recommendation: build a free app in an underserved but high-demand category (like books/reference), launch first on Google Play to maximise reach, then expand to the App Store once validated.

The analysis was done in Python using pandas, and the full notebook is available on my GitHub.
